Welcome to my website! I'm a journalist, writer, developer and project manager with more than 23 years of experience at The Times.

I have a wide array of skills — to learn a bit more detail about a particular skill, select the entry in the navigation bar above.

I'll be periodically updating this page to showcase the web developement skills I'm learning.

Developer

I'm just taking my baby steps as a developer — at this point I'm taking classes at Codecademy to learn HTML, CSS, Javascript and jQuery.

I don't have ambitions to be a full-stack developer — rather, I'm taking these classes to develop just enough fluency in web development to be conversant with the amazing developers I work with.

It's also my ambition to get to a level where I can prototype new useful news products — for example, the Story Type Input Form I'm working on.

Journalist

I've never wavered in my commitment to journalism — it's the only job I've ever held.

I came to The Times directly out of college and immediately joined the News Clerical staff.

This turned out to be the ideal first job here — I quickly became a floater, plugging a variety of holes in every capacity, from administrative to technical to creative.

From there, I joined the electronic pagination team, which soon led to a technical support role.

It was in that role that I truly discovered where I wanted to develop an expertise — in helping people quickly develop a level of comfort with intimidating technologies.

After three years spent honing my technical and organizational skills as a project manager for our CMS group, I moved back to the newsroom to my dream job as Editor for Tools and Technology as part of the newsroom's Digital Transition Team.

In this new role, I use my technical, trainer and project skills to roll out new software, developing training programs, partnering with developer teams, training new users, and supporting them during rollout to set them up for success.
