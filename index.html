About

Welcome to my website! I'm a journalist, writer, developer, program manager, project manager, and veteran software trainer with more than 23 years of experience at The New York Times.

To learn a bit more detail about my areas of specialization, select the entry in the navigation bar above.

I'll be periodically updating this page to showcase the web developement skills I'm learning.

Journalist

I've spent more than two decades working for The New York Times.

This sounds like a narrow path, until you consider to extremely wide variety of positions I've held at The Times.

I've worked in just about every capacity imaginable — news and feature writing, graphic design, news design, software training, technical support, software piloting, content strategy, analytics, program management, project management, and workflow management.

Currently, I'm working in a collaborative role as Editor, Tools and Technology, as part of the newsroom's Digital Transition initiative, providing the newsroom with the training, education and support our journalists need to migrate to a truly digital-first ecosystem.

Developer

I'm just taking my baby steps as a developer — at this point I'm taking classes at Codecademy to learn HTML, CSS, Javascript and jQuery.

I don't have ambitions to be a full-stack developer — rather, I'm taking these classes to develop just enough fluency in web development to be conversant with the amazing developers I work with.

It's also my ambition to get to a level where I can prototype new useful news products — for example, the Story Type Input Form I'm working on.

Product Owner

As an extension of my project management experience, in recent years I've begun to adopt product owner roles for some of my more high-profile projects

The most significant of these was for the Times Oak Editor project. I assumed a product owner role for the project, using data-driven, customer-informed decisions to drive the tool's development and roadmap.
